Visit Brum

Your 1st destination to plan your 2nd city stay

Birmingham is the UK's second largest City. At the heart of the industrial revolution, Birminghams growth exploded in the 18th century turning a small market town into a bustling city of more than 1 million people. Managing to avoid the gaze of many tourists Birmingham is the perfect place to come experience the true multicultural reality that is the 21st century UK

Jewellery Quarter


The Jewellery Quarter is famous of course for its jewellery (in fact 1/2 of all jewellery sold in the UK is made here), but also for its redbrick warehouses and chilled out eateries and bars. Where better to enjoy a drink after a day of sight seeing than in the sun on one of the many rooftop terraces watching the world go by below.

City Centre


The shopping hub of central England. Birminghams famous Bullring shopping centre mixes high fashion with metropolitan living. Meanwhile within a stones throw are multiple Michellin Starred restaurants and more bars than a short stay would allow you to explore.

Digbeth


Having carried the tag of 'Up and coming' for many years Digbeth has recently been voted best place to live in the UK. Once the industrial heartbeat of Birmingham Digbeth is now better known for its thriving art scene. Here you can admire the grafitti art while traversing the many bars, micro-breweries and street food outlets now inhabiting the old warehouse spaces.

Where to Stay

Jewellery Quarter

This leafy district of town has many gems hidden among the warehouses, get away from the crowds and noise in the Jewellery Quarter (click to find out more)

Bloc Hotel (A) blochotels.com/

Brand new and overlooking one of Birminghams leafiest boulevards opposite Otto Pizza, this is a chic hotel without the usual cost.

St Pauls House (B) saintpaulshouse.com/

Newly revamped St Paul's House sits right on St Pauls Square. With bars less than a minute walk away what more do you need?

Frederick Street Town House (C) frederickstreettownhouse.co.uk/

A glorious Georgian townhouse in the heart of the Jewellery quarter. Seconds walk away from the famed diamond dealers this really is the place to be seen.

City Centre

Up market and right in the hustle and bustle of a living city (click to find out more)

Grand Hotel (J) thegrandbirmingham.co.uk/

Recently refurbished with a rooftop pool the Grand Hotel overlooks Birmingham Cathedral.

Britannia Hotel (K) britanniahotels.com/

What the Britannia lacks in ammenities it makes up in value. Just think of what more extra you can buy with the money you've saved!

Macdonalds Burlington (L) macdonaldhotels.co.uk/

5 star Luxury the Macdonalds Burlington sits above the ultra exclusive Burlington Arcade, with mega suites and on-site spa make sure you find time to get out and explore!.

Digbeth

On a budget or looking for atmosphere over luxury, staying in Digbeth puts you right in the centre of Birminghams most creative district (click to find out more)

Birmingham Backpackers (S) birminghambackpackers.com/

Value Value Value. Right in the heart of Digbeth you aren't paying for creature comforts rather great atmosphere and company.

Ladbrooke Hotel (T)no website

Dont fancy sharing a Dormatory? The Ladbrooke Hotel is luxury you don't normally find this side of town at a reasonable price.



Where to eat and drink

Jewellery Quarter

Moderately priced restaurants and a plethora of chilled out bars, the Jewellery Quarter is perfect to relax (click to find out more)

The Button Factory (D) thebuttonfactorybirmingham.co.uk/

With its rooftop terrace where better to sit and watch the world go by.

40 St Pauls (E) 40stpauls.co.uk/

Recently voted world's best gin bar come and sip on a gin an tonic while looking out over the picturesque St Paul's Square.

The Rectory (F) therectorybirmingham.co.uk/

Bottomless brunch... need we continue? Situated in the opposite corner of St Paul's Square this is a must visit.

Otto Pizza (G) ottowoodfired.com/

World class Italian pizza situated on the side of one of the Jewellery Quarters longest tree lined boulevards.

Lasan (H) lasan.co.uk/

Birmingham is famous for its Indian food. Multiple award winners this restaurant is the most affordable jewel in the Jewellery Quarter .

City Centre

Fine dining and cocktail bars. The City Centre is where Birmingham comes alive at night (click to find out more)

Adam's (M) adamsrestaurant.co.uk/

The first Michellin starred restaurant on this list, Adam's is a cullinary delight.

Sushi Passion (N) sushipassion.has.restaurant/

Two worlds collide here where you can get world class sushi served in a traditional Victorian arcade.

Old Joint Stock (O) oldjointstock.co.uk/

This grand old building used to be the local stock exchange, now a gastro pub whilst still holding onto the traditional decor you can enjoy beers from all over the world in the most wonderful surroundings

Purnell's (P) purnellsrestaurant.com/

The second Michellin starred restaurant belongs to famed brummie Glynn Purnell. Known for adventurous food while adding a hint of Birmingham this is a must visit.

Digbeth

Cheap drinks and street food, what more could you want (click to find out more)

Rico Libre (M) ricolibre.co.uk/

The best tapas in the whole of Birmingham. Come here for the food, stay for the free salsa lessons.

Warehouse Cafe (N) thewarehousecafe.com/

This quirky vegan restaurant has been serving hungry brummies for over a decade, it's longevity a testiment to its quality

Digbeth Dining Club (O) digbethdiningclub.com/

Award winning street food, world class DJs. Every Thursday to Sunday locals congregate here to have food and be merry.

Dig Brew Co. (P) digbrewco.com/

Drink beer mere feet away from where it was brewed at Dig Brew Co. This micro brewery serves up its award winning home brews at lower than low prices.




Things to do

Jewellery Quarter

Get lost in the warren of side alleys and window shop at the many worlds class jewellers(click to find out more)

The Pen Museum (I) penmuseum.org.uk/

This wonderful locally run museum proves the pen really is mightier than the sword. Take a journey from the quill to the ballpoint and have loads of fun along the way!

City Centre

Take in some culture at the many museums or shop 'til you drop(click to find out more)

Birmingham Museum (Q) birminghammuseums.org.uk/

Priceless art for free! The Birmigham museum brings together art of all eras together, from Rembrandt to Emin, and it doesn't cost a penny.

The Bullring (R) bullring.co.uk/

A huge 3 story inner city shopping haven. The Bullring brings together designer brands with local retailers for the ultimate shopping experience.

A wild blend of urban culture and cheap thrills Digbeth is Birminghams playground(click to find out more)

Ghetto Golf (Y) ghettogolf.co.uk/

With an inhouse bar and Reggae Music this wont be your orginary round of golf.

Go-Karting (Z) teamworkskarting.com/

Fancy Something a bit more hi-octane? Burn some rubber and battle it out to be the fastest.
